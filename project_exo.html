Hybrid Neural Exoskeleton

/// SYSTEM OVERVIEW

Hybrid Neuro-Muscular Interface

The Mk I platform enables accessible human augmentation by fusing rapid-response Electromyography (EMG) with cognitive state monitoring (EEG). Unlike cost-prohibitive industrial systems, the Mk I utilizes a distributed servo architecture to achieve shoulder-to-finger articulation.

The structural core combines 3D Printed PETG topology-optimized parts with Aluminum Flat Bar reinforcement, creating a chassis that is both affordable to manufacture and rigid enough to support high-torque actuation at the shoulder joint.

Control Architecture

To overcome the latency of pure brain-computer interfaces (BCI), the Mk I employs a hybrid approach:

  • EEG Gating (NeuroSky): Monitors user "Attention" levels. The system only unlocks torque-assist when concentration exceeds 70%, preventing accidental movement.
  • EMG Actuation (MyoWare): Reads pre-actuation muscle potentials from the bicep and forearm to drive motors with sub-20ms latency.

Development Roadmap

Current fabrication is focused on the "Gauntlet" sub-assembly. We are utilizing MG90S Micro Servos for the finger tendons to refine the "Power Grip" algorithm before integrating the heavier shoulder lift mechanism.
